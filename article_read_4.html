TIPS FOR CLEAN EATING WITH A LIMITED BUDGET

One of the challenges we can face in adapting to a healthy lifestyle is weighing the cost of our food bill. Sometimes the high-quality food options aren't the cheapest products to purchase and clean eating can be difficult to manage - especially if you're studying or working part-time.

WHAT EXACTLY IS 'CLEAN EATING'?

Clean eating refers to eating minimally processed, nutritious products that have no additives - foods such as fresh vegetables and legumes, whole grains, fruits, and lean meats. Unfortunately, the thing that can often keep us from buying these higher quality foods is cost - good quality healthy products are often more expensive than less healthy alternatives, especially when labeled as 'organic'. However, clean eating on a budget is possible! It just takes a little research, planning ahead, and sticking to your meal plans.

Do you want to know how to eat clean without spending all your money? Follow these simple tips to help you eat a healthy diet and stay on track with your budget.

TIP 1. CHOOSE YOUR FOOD STORE WISELY

Have you ever walked into a food store to see the same food you recently bought, for half the price? While this can be very frustrating, there is a way to use it to your advantage. Do some research by checking out nearby farmers' markets, health food stores, small food chains and commercial stores - then compare their prices. You will eventually learn which one offers the best quality and bang for your buck so you can choose to buy food there more often

The type of food stores available to you will depend on where you live, but the more research you do before committing to a regular store, the better. Sometimes your most convenient is less budget-friendly than alternatives and can cost you more in the long run.

TIP 2. CHOOSE CHEAPER BRANDS

Generic brands often contain products of the same quality, but can be much cheaper. Remember that more expensive does not always mean better quality! Sometimes the appearance and taste can vary from brand to brand, but for 'clean' food this is often minimal.

TIP 3. INGREDIENTS ARE IMPORTANT!

Look for seasonal fruits and vegetables available in your area - they are likely to be more expensive if sold outside of their natural season. Do you need a prescription for products that are not in season where you live? Try to swap the ingredients for something similar that is in season. Your dish will probably taste better for it too, because the ingredients are at their best!

TIP 4. DON'T BE GIVEN BY "ORGANIC" LABELS

Sometimes a product that appears organic (based on words or images on the packaging) is actually not! Take care when choosing items that appear 'organic' as this term can sometimes be used to make a product appear healthier than its competition, when it is not officially certified as an organic product. In order to be certified as organic, an extensive process actually has to take place where the business is audited by a third party organic certification company.

Also, some foods are generally produced with fewer (or no) chemicals, so buying a more expensive "organic" version may not necessarily be worth it. The key is to do some research on how your food is produced and then take this into account while shopping. This way, you can choose organic when you need it, not because the label promotes it that way.

TIP 5. BULK IS THE BEST!

If you know that a food is something you eat often and will last a long time, buy it in bulk. For example, large bottles of healthy oils and grains are good to buy in bulk as you will likely use them a lot when preparing 'clean' food. When you first start to change your diet, it may feel like your food bill is higher than normal. In the first few shopping trips, you buy pantry staples that will last. However, once your pantry is stocked, you'll find that groceries are getting cheaper and you may not have to go as often!

TIP 6. BE SMART WITH YOUR MEAL PREPARATION

After you go to a grocery store, you don't want to have so much food left that it starts to go bad before you can cook it. To avoid this, be prepared before shopping! Make a list of the meals you will eat in the week before you go shopping, noting any social events you have during that week. Then determine which ingredients you need for each meal. If you plan meals according to recipes, consider the number of serves and decide if this is realistic for your week.

When it comes to cooking, you can also use all your ingredients and prepare ahead of time. If a recipe says it serves two, use the extra serves as a meal for another day. Sometimes, even if you limit the number of servings in a recipe, you may still have to buy all the food you would like to make the whole lot. Rather than getting wasted, prepping the recipe in full means eating meals for other days and can save you money in the long run!

TIP 7. SAVE WHAT YOU CAN

Sometimes plans change and you realize you don't have a chance to eat all the fruits and vegetables you bought. Spend some time chopping them up, seal them in zippered containers or bags, and keep in the freezer. These can then be used roadside for meals such as smoothies or stir fry.

EATING CLEAN DOES NOT HAVE TO BREAK YOUR BUDGET

Eating clean doesn't have to mean spending all your hard-earned money on the most expensive organic products or superfoods. Sometimes it only takes a few small changes to save you some serious money and stay healthy.
